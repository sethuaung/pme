Mastering the Digital Crime Scene

An immersive journey through the Felixent Institute's Digital Forensics & Incident Response (DFIR) curriculum, designed to forge elite cyber investigators.

The Core Curriculum: A 7-Module Deep Dive

Module 1: Foundations of Digital Forensics

This initial module establishes the bedrock of knowledge for all future investigations. It covers the essential principles, legal frameworks, and ethical guidelines that govern the practice of digital forensics, ensuring every action taken is sound, defensible, and professional.

  • DFIR Concepts & Terminology
  • Legal and Ethical Considerations
  • Types of Digital Evidence
  • Chain of Custody and Documentation
⬇

Module 2: Operating Systems & File Systems

Investigators delve into the digital environments where evidence resides. This module provides a comprehensive understanding of how data is stored, managed, and hidden within the most common operating systems, from file structures and metadata to the techniques for recovering deleted information.

  • Windows, Linux, and macOS Forensics
  • File System Structures (NTFS, EXT4, APFS)
  • Metadata and Timestamp Analysis
  • Hidden and Deleted File Recovery
⬇

Module 3: Forensic Tools & Techniques

Theory is put into practice as students gain hands-on experience with the industry-standard tools of the trade. This module focuses on the practical application of software and methodologies for acquiring, preserving, and analyzing digital evidence from a variety of sources, including volatile memory and network traffic.

  • Open-Source Tools: Autopsy, Sleuth Kit, Volatility
  • Imaging, Hashing, and Data Carving
  • Memory Forensics & Volatile Data Capture
  • Network Forensics Basics
⬇

Module 4: Incident Response Strategy

Beyond forensics, this module covers the high-stakes process of responding to live security incidents. Students learn the structured phases of incident response, from initial preparation and detection to final recovery and reporting, equipping them to manage crises effectively.

  • Phases of Incident Response
  • Log Analysis and SIEM Integration
  • Threat Intelligence and IOC Correlation
  • Reporting and Post-Incident Review
⬇

Module 5: Mobile & Cloud Forensics

The investigation expands to modern platforms where critical data increasingly resides. This section addresses the unique challenges and techniques for extracting and analyzing evidence from mobile devices and cloud services, a critical skill in today's interconnected world.

  • Android and iOS Data Acquisition
  • Cloud Service Artifacts (AWS, Google Drive)
  • Challenges in Remote Evidence Collection
⬇

Module 6 & 7: Application & Certification

The final modules synthesize all learned skills through real-world case studies and a comprehensive capstone project. The curriculum culminates in preparing students for key industry certifications, validating their expertise and launching their careers in DFIR.

  • Real-World Case Walkthroughs
  • Legal Frameworks and Compliance
  • Capstone Project Execution
  • Certification Pathways (GCFA, CHFI)

The Investigator's Toolkit

Students gain proficiency in a wide range of forensic tools, each serving a specific purpose in the investigative process. The curriculum emphasizes a balanced skill set across different domains of digital evidence analysis.

Capstone: Insider Threat Simulation

The capstone project challenges students to apply their knowledge to a realistic scenario involving data exfiltration by a malicious insider. This project tests every aspect of their training, from evidence acquisition to final reporting.
