Originating in Great Britain, the First Industrial Revolution marked a fundamental shift from manual labor to machine-based manufacturing. The Second Industrial Revolution saw industrialization spread across Europe, North America, and Japan. This period was characterized by the harnessing of electricity, the invention of the internal combustion engine, and the development of mass production techniques. These advancements didn't just improve upon old methods; they created entirely new industries and possibilities.

The comparison chart illustrates how the focus of innovation shifted, building upon the mechanical foundations of the first wave with advancements in chemicals, large-scale manufacturing, and global communication.
