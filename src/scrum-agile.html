Introduction to Scrum & Agile

A new way to build amazing things, faster and better.

What is Agile?

Agile is a mindset and a set of principles for developing products. Instead of one big launch, Agile focuses on delivering value in small, iterative cycles. This allows teams to adapt to change, get feedback early, and build what customers truly want. It's about collaboration, flexibility, and continuous improvement.

Agile Adoption is Skyrocketing

Modern teams overwhelmingly prefer Agile methods over traditional Waterfall approaches due to their flexibility and efficiency.

The Three Scrum Artifacts

Tools to provide transparency of key information.

1. Product Backlog

An ordered list of everything that is known to be needed in the product. It is the single source of requirements for any changes to be made to the product. It's a living artifact, constantly evolving.

2. Sprint Backlog

The set of Product Backlog items selected for the Sprint, plus a plan for delivering the product Increment. It's a highly visible, real-time picture of the work that the Developers plan to accomplish during the Sprint.

3. Increment

The sum of all the Product Backlog items completed during a Sprint and the value of the increments of all previous Sprints. At the end of a Sprint, the new Increment must be "Done," which means it is in a usable condition.

Example Scenario: Recipe App

Let's apply Scrum to building a simple mobile recipe app in one 2-week sprint.

The Goal for Sprint 1

"Launch a basic version of the app where users can search for recipes and view their details."

Sprint Backlog (Selected User Stories)

  • As a user, I want to create an account so I can save favorite recipes. (8 points)
  • As a user, I want a search bar to find recipes by name. (5 points)
  • As a user, I want to see a list of ingredients for a recipe. (3 points)
  • As a user, I want to read step-by-step instructions. (3 points)

Total Sprint Points: 19

Sprint Progress: Burndown Chart

This chart tracks the remaining work (in story points) throughout the 10-day sprint. The team started with 19 points of work.

Sprint Outcome (Answer)

At the Sprint Review, the team demonstrates the finished features. The Increment is a working app where users can now successfully search for a recipe and view its ingredients and instructions. The "create account" feature was not fully completed and will be moved back to the Product Backlog for a future sprint.
