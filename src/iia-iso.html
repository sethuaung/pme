No single audit model fits all organizations. The choice of model depends on factors like organizational maturity, industry, and risk appetite. This section provides an interactive comparison of the most common approaches.

Risk-Based Auditing (RBA)

The foundation of modern internal audit. RBA aligns audit activities with the organization's most significant risks. It is a top-down approach that ensures resources are focused where they can add the most value by addressing strategic threats.
