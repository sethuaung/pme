How We'll Achieve the ROI

The **$300K** revenue increase is a direct result of the CRM's impact on our core KPIs, primarily driven by improved **customer retention** and **booking conversion**.

  • **Increased Customer Retention:** By targeting a 15% increase in retention (from 60% to 75%), we expect to generate a significant portion of the projected revenue from repeat business, as loyal customers are more likely to book with us again.
  • **Improved Booking Conversion:** Automating follow-ups and personalizing offers with the CRM aims to boost our conversion rate by 5% (from 10% to 15%). This means more leads turning into successful bookings, driving new revenue.
  • **The Calculation:** The ROI is calculated by dividing the net profit (Revenue Increase - Initial Investment) by the initial investment:
    ($300,000 - $120,000) / $120,000 = 1.5
    This is then expressed as a percentage: 1.5 * 100% = 150%. Wait, that's not right. The ROI is just a simple ratio, a ratio of gain/cost.
    It's calculated by `(Total Revenue / Total Investment) * 100 = ROI%` or `(Net Profit / Initial Investment) * 100 = ROI%`. In our case the return is the projected revenue increase, so we use `(Projected Revenue Increase / Initial Investment) * 100 = ROI%`
    so, `($300,000/$120,000)*100 = 250%`. That makes more sense. My apologies.
